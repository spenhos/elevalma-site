## The Book Without God's Name

Meguilat Esther is the only book in the Tanakh where God's name doesn't appear. Not once. And yet — the entire story is about divine orchestration. Every "coincidence" is a thread in a fabric woven by an invisible hand.

Esther becomes queen — coincidence? Mordechai overhears a plot — coincidence? The king can't sleep on *that* specific night — coincidence?

Purim teaches us to read the hidden narrative behind the visible one.

## Hester Panim — The Hidden Face

The name "Esther" itself comes from *hester* — concealment. The Talmud hints at this when it asks: where is Esther in the Torah? And answers with the verse *"Anokhi haster astir panai"* — "I will surely hide My face."

God doesn't disappear on Purim. He puts on a mask. And the entire holiday is about learning to see behind the mask — in history, in others, and in yourself.

## The Reversal

*V'nahafokh hu* — and it was reversed. Everything that was meant for destruction became salvation. The day chosen for annihilation became a day of celebration.

This is the deepest teaching of Purim: the worst thing that happens to you might be the setup for the greatest reversal of your life. But you can only see it in retrospect.

## Seeing Behind the Mask

What in your life looks like random chaos but might be divine choreography? What "coincidence" are you dismissing that could be the hidden hand of God?

Purim invites you to look again — with different eyes. Not the eyes of logic, but the eyes of trust. Because sometimes the most profound revelation comes in the deepest disguise.
